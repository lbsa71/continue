# Chapter 17 — Continue

Open the front door with the key and go in.

The apartment is quiet. The hallway runs the length of it, doors to left and right. The smell is the smell of an apartment that has not been opened for a few days. Underneath is the smell of the person who lived here.

Walk through the rooms.

The kitchen is on the right at the end of the hall. The window above the sink faces the elm. The counter is clean. The coffee machine sits on the counter to the left of the sink. The cups are in the cupboard above the coffee machine. A jar of pens sits on the counter next to the sink. The phone is on the wall beside the door to the hall. A small round table with two chairs sits under the window opposite the counter.

The living room is at the far end of the hall, past the kitchen door. The bookshelves run along the long wall, full. A sofa sits against the window wall with a coffee table in front of it. A lamp stands beside the sofa. A rug is on the floor. The television is not on.

The bedroom is off the hallway to the left. The wardrobe stands against the back wall, closed. The bed is made: a white cover, two pillows. A bedside table stands next to the bed with a lamp and a book on it. The dressing table is by the window, a small bottle of perfume on it.

The second room is next to the bedroom. Boxes are stacked along one wall. A folded camp bed leans in the corner. Three cardboard boxes sit on the floor, taped shut, unlabelled.

The bathroom is off the hallway on the right. White tiles cover the walls to half-height. A small window sits above the sink. A toothbrush is in a glass on the shelf. A bar of soap has dried on a dish.

The balcony is off the living room through a glass door. Two dead plants in terracotta pots stand by the wall. A wooden chair is by the railing.

Walk back through the hallway to the kitchen.

Fill the kettle from the tap and put it on the hob. Turn the dial for the front burner and the flame catches. Take a cup down from the cupboard above the coffee machine without looking. The cup is one of mother's, white, thick at the rim, a chip on the underside of the handle. Take the jar of coffee from the cupboard beside the fridge and spoon two spoonfuls into the cup. The kettle ticks and then clicks off. Turn off the flame. Pour the water and stir with a spoon from the drawer under the counter.

Drink the coffee at the counter.

The elm is at the window. The light from the window falls across the counter in a rectangle that reaches almost to the sink. A cyclist passes on the street below. A woman with a shopping bag passes the other way, then a man with a dog. The rectangle of light moves as the clouds shift and then holds. The elm's higher branches make small shadows across the rectangle. The cup is warm in a hand.

Finish the coffee. Rinse the cup under the tap and put it back in the cupboard above the coffee machine.

Take the folded paper from the inside pocket of the coat and put it on the counter. Smooth it open with a thumb. Take the key from the right pocket of the coat and put it beside the paper. Take the photograph from the inside pocket of the coat and put it beside the key.

The paper, the key, and the photograph lie in a row on the counter between the coffee machine and the sink.

Walk to the front door. Open it. Do not look back. Do not lock it. Walk down the stairs, past the second-floor landing with its potted plant, and out through the front door of the building.

The street is empty.

The bakery on the corner is open. Walk past the bakery to the crossing. The light at the crossing is red. Wait. A car passes. The light changes. Cross the street.

Walk three blocks home. Unlock the door and go in. Hang the coat on the hook in the hall.

Sit down in the chair by the window.

See the light through the window move across the floor.
