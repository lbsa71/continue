# Chapter 3 — The Utility Company

Unlock the apartment door with the key from the right pocket of the coat and go in. The hallway runs the length of it, doors to left and right. Drop the key back into the pocket. Hang the coat on the hook by the door.

The kitchen. The counter. The phone on the wall beside the door to the hall. Take the mobile from the inside pocket of the coat on the hook and dial the number of the utility company from the top of the last bill.

The line rings and then a recorded voice asks for the account number. Press 1 for English. Press 2 to speak to an operator. Hold.

The hold music is a single piece of light piano, looping. Stand at the counter and look out at the elm while the music loops. After four loops, an operator picks up.

The operator asks for the account number.

The account number is not on the mobile. It is on a bill. The bills are in a folder in the apartment.

Tell the operator to hold on. Put the mobile on the counter and walk through the hallway to find the folder.

The folder is in the bedroom. Walk through the kitchen to the bedroom. The folder is on top of the dressing table by the window, leaning against the small bottle of perfume. Take the folder and the bill at the front of it back to the kitchen counter.

Read the account number off the bill to the operator. The operator types. The operator reads back the account number. Confirm.

The operator asks for the name on the account.

Read the name at the top of the bill to the operator. The name has an initial between the first name and the last name. The initial is not the one that should be there.

The operator says the name on the account has a slightly different initial and asks whether this is the right account.

Say yes.

The operator says the notice of death has been received. The operator says the final bill has been calculated and will be sent to the forwarding address on file. The operator asks whether the forwarding address is still correct.

Look at the bill. The forwarding address is the apartment. Say yes.

The operator says the account will be closed at the end of the month and wishes a good day. Thank the operator. End the call.

Put the mobile back in the inside pocket of the coat on the hook. Take the folder to the small round table under the kitchen window and sit down to go through the rest of the papers.

The folder contains utility bills in plastic sleeves, six months of them, the name with the extra initial on every one, a warranty card for a radio, a receipt for a pair of shoes from two years ago, a handwritten note listing the dates of three dentist appointments, and a library notice saying a book is overdue, the fine small, the title not one that stops the hand.

Close the folder and put it back on the dressing table. Take the library notice off the folder and set it on the counter beside the door to the hall.

Walk back through the kitchen to the hallway and check the small table by the front door where things are sometimes left. The library book is on the table, a hardback with a paper jacket. Put the book on the floor next to the door with the library notice on top of it.

The list is in the inside pocket of the coat on the hook. Take it out and read it at the counter. Item 2 — *Cancel utilities* — has a line drawn through it. The line is in the same pen and the same hand as the rest of the list. No hand drew the line at the table. Read the list to the end. Fold the paper once and then once again and put it back in the inside pocket of the coat.

Put the coat on. Pick up the library book and the notice from the floor. Take the key from the right pocket of the coat. Pull the apartment door closed and turn the key in the lock. Drop the key back into the pocket.

Walk down the stairs, past the second-floor landing with its potted plant, and out through the front door of the building.

The library is four blocks from the apartment, past the bakery, across the crossing, along the street with the lime trees. Walk to the library with the book under one arm and the notice in a hand.

The library is quiet. Two people read at tables by the window. The librarian at the returns desk is older, a woman, with glasses on a chain around her neck.

Put the book and the notice on the desk. The librarian picks up the book and scans the barcode. The librarian looks at the date on the screen and then at the face above the desk and then back at the screen.

The librarian says the fine is waived for today. The librarian stamps the notice with *RETURNED* and hands it back.

Thank the librarian.

Walk back through the library to the door, past the two readers, out onto the street.

The street is empty.
