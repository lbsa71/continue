## Revision Notes

### v0.1 — First-pass bookends (Pass 1)

**Objective:** Establish the baseline voice in Chapter 1 and the restored cadence in Chapter 17, so that the middle chapters have anchors to calibrate against.

**Approach:**
- Chapter 1 drafted end-to-end in one session.
- Chapter 17 drafted end-to-end in the same session immediately after.
- Both chapters use identical phrasing for the kitchen walkthrough, the cup description, and the rectangle-of-light sentence.
- Chapter 17 includes a single rule 11 efficiency marker (*Take a cup down from the cupboard above the coffee machine without looking.*) that Chapter 1 does not include. This is the only intentional divergence in the kitchen-and-coffee sequences.

**Known issues to address in revision:**
- Ch 1 word count (~975) close to target. Ch 17 word count (~770) below the 900 minimum. Ch 17 may benefit from slight expansion during revision — candidates: the walk home, the moment at the counter placing the three objects, the arrival at the protagonist's own apartment.
- Quality gate for cadence match not yet run. See "Next Actions" below.
- The inscription text for Chapter 12 not yet drafted; to be done before Pass 2 begins.

**Protected passages (DO NOT shorten in future revision):**
- The fourteen items of the original list in Ch 1 (must be quoted verbatim)
- The phone sentence in Ch 1 (*The phone on the kitchen wall is black, the kind with a cord...*)
- The rectangle of light sentence in Ch 1 and Ch 17 (*The light from the window falls across the counter in a rectangle that reaches almost to the sink.*)
- The closing refrain *The street is empty.* in both chapters
- The final sentence *The light through the window moves across the floor.*

## Notes and Observations

The voice held without visible effort during the Ch 1 and Ch 17 drafting. The bare-verb imperative mode reads naturally once it is calibrated, and the refusal of pronouns for the protagonist is now internalised enough that it does not require constant vigilance. The one passage that required multiple attempts was the "one sentence too many" about the phone at the end of Chapter 1 — several versions were tried before settling on the canonical form. The five-clause sentence with the cord coiled once around the hook below the handset feels right because each clause is a slightly unnecessary addition, and the final clause (*where mother left it*) is the quiet place the sentence has been approaching.

Chapter 17's echo of Chapter 1 is at this point almost verbatim in the kitchen-walkthrough and coffee-making sequences. This may turn out to be too on-the-nose; during revision, some Ch 17 phrasings may need slight variation to keep the echo from reading as copy-paste. But for the first draft the verbatim echo is the safer choice — it guarantees the cadence match that Pass 1's whole purpose is to establish.
